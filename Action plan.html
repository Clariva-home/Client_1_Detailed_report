Week 01 · Days 1–7
Margin & Measurement Foundation
Before you scale anything, you must know what you're scaling.

Step 01
Install the Clariva Measurement Stack
Complexity: Low
▼
The Action
Go to Shopify Analytics → Reports → Customers → Retention Analysis. Map the % of customers who bought in January 2026 and returned by March 2026. If Shopify reports feel insufficient, install Lifetimely (free tier available) to track 90-day LTV cohorts automatically.
Outcome: Baseline LTV visibility established
Critical Watch Point
Isolate the "Returning Customer Rate" specifically for the Repair Oil SKU — this is your LTV driver. Do not aggregate across all products. A blended rate will hide the Oil's true retention signal.
Step 02
Device-Split Measurement Setup
Complexity: Low
▼
The Action
Open Google Analytics (GA4) or Shopify Reports. Filter your Conversion Rate by "Device Category." Look at the last 90 days for statistical significance. The diagnostic signal: if Desktop CVR is 4% and Mobile CVR is 1.2%, you have a confirmed technical/UX bottleneck on phones — not a traffic quality problem.
Outcome: Mobile vs desktop CVR gap quantified
What This Unlocks
This data point is the single most important number in your diagnostic stack. Without it, you cannot distinguish between "bad traffic" and "bad page experience." Most D2C founders at your revenue band are blind to this gap and mistakenly blame their creative agency.
Step 03
Mobile UX Friction Test & Fixes
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
Perform a "Live Buy" test on both Android and iPhone. Complete the full purchase journey as a cold visitor. Then implement the following fixes:
  • Sticky ATC: Ensure "Add to Cart" is always visible at screen bottom as the user scrolls — this alone can lift mobile CVR by 0.3–0.5%
  • Review Visibility: Move star ratings and customer photos directly under the product title — above the fold on mobile
  • Pop-up Cleanse: Disable newsletter pop-ups that cover the full mobile screen for cold traffic — they kill conversion at the worst moment
Outcome: Mobile conversion friction reduced
Critical Watch Point
Requires a Shopify developer or theme settings adjustment. If your developer is unavailable, use PageFly or Shogun to rearrange blocks without code. Do not let this step wait — mobile UX is the single highest-leverage fix available at zero media cost.
Step 04
Eco-Lite Shipping Protocol
Complexity: Low
▼
The Action
Order plain, high-quality corrugated boxes for first-time orders. Reserve the "Intentional Unboxing" (custom prints, ribbons, extras) strictly for second-order customers and above. This is a procurement shift, not a quality downgrade.

Add a small printed note inside Order 1: "We invest in your ingredients, not the trash. Expect a special surprise in your next order." This manages expectations while teasing the next purchase.
Outcome: ₹40–50 saved per acquisition order
The Economics
At your current scale of ~1,000 orders/month, saving ₹45 per first-time order generates approximately ₹45,000/month in pure profit recovery — before a single additional sale. That recovered margin can be directly reinvested into Meta spend for organic growth compounding.
Step 05
WhatsApp COD Verification Flow
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
Set up an automated message via Interakt or BiteSpeed that triggers the moment a COD order is placed. The order enters a "Pending Confirmation" state. It is only marked "Confirmed" once the customer replies with "1" or a one-time PIN.

The message template: "Your Dusk & Ritual order #XXXX is placed! To confirm dispatch, reply 1. No reply within 4 hours = auto-cancelled."
Outcome: RTO drops from 30% to <15%
Critical Watch Point
Requires setting up a flow in your WhatsApp tool. Allow 2–3 days for setup and testing. Do not launch on full COD volume immediately — test with 20% of COD orders for 48 hours first to catch edge cases. The RTO reduction alone recovers approximately ₹15,000–₹20,000/month in wasted logistics cost.

Step 06
The Hero Shift in Meta Ads
Complexity: Low
▼
The Action
In your Meta Ads Manager: turn off all ad sets currently landing on the Starter Kit PDP. Redirect 100% of Top-of-Funnel (TOF) budget to the Repair Oil PDP. This is a dashboard change — no new creative required at this stage.

The data rationale: Repair Oil has a 14% ATC rate vs. 7% for the Starter Kit. You are currently paying Meta to send people to the path that converts at half the rate.
Outcome: Site-wide CVR moves 2% → 3.5%+
Critical Watch Point
Ensure the existing ad creative specifically highlights the "30-Day Transformation" of the Oil — not just the ingredients. If your current creative focuses on ceramide science, pause it and run a simple UGC-style before/after video. Creative-offer alignment is as important as the offer itself.
Step 07
Impact-First PDP Rebuild
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
Edit the Repair Oil product page with a strict above/below fold discipline:
  • Remove: "Ceramide Science" text from the hero section
  • Replace with: A Before/After visual slider + headline: "Waking up to glowing skin isn't a ritual, it's a result."
  • Add: A "7-day Impact" stat block and 3–4 customer photo reviews — all visible without scrolling
  • Move below fold: "How it works" + full ingredient education section
Outcome: Faster ATC · Lower mobile bounce rate
Critical Watch Point
After every PDP edit, check the mobile preview in Shopify before publishing. The "Add to Cart" button must be visible without a single scroll on mobile. This is non-negotiable — your traffic is majority mobile and the button visibility gap alone accounts for a material CVR deficit.
Week 03 · Days 15–21
The Retention Engine

Focus: Capture
the 45-day cycle.
Step 08
Launch the 45-Day Replenishment Loop
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
Set up an automated WhatsApp/Email trigger keyed to the purchase date of any 30ml SKU. Two-message sequence:
  • Day 35 (Value): "How's the glow? You should be halfway through your Repair Oil by now. Consistency is the real ritual."
  • Day 42 (Conversion): "Don't let your skin go hungry. Your bottle is likely empty. Refill today and get 10% off your next order."
This sequence converts your existing customer base into zero-ad-cost revenue.
Outcome: +10–15% revenue from automated repeats
Critical Watch Points
Requires syncing Shopify order dates with your communication tool (Klaviyo / BiteSpeed / Interakt). Exclude customers who have already repurchased within that window — sending a replenishment discount to someone who just paid full price destroys trust.

The 10% discount on Day 42 is a margin-positive trade. Recovering a lapsed customer at ₹116 off is infinitely cheaper than re-acquiring them via Meta at ₹400+ CAC.
Step 09
Trial-to-Routine Upsell (Starter Kit Repositioning)
Complexity: Low
▼
The Action
Remove the Starter Kit from all acquisition ad creative. Reposition it exclusively as a "Post-Purchase Cross-sell."

Tactic: 5 minutes after a customer buys the Repair Oil, trigger an email/WhatsApp:
"Want to complete the ritual? Add the full Starter Kit — Moisturiser + Cleanser — for ₹X extra. Added to your order if claimed in the next 30 minutes."
Outcome: AOV protected without hurting TOF CVR
Why This Works
The Starter Kit fails as an acquisition offer because strangers reject high-priced trials. But existing buyers — who have already made a trust decision — are dramatically more likely to expand. You preserve AOV without using a friction point as your first impression.

Step 10
The MER Scaling Test
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
Calculate your new Net Contribution Margin after the Week 1 packaging and RTO fixes. Then:
  • If CVR has improved from 2% to 3%+ (confirmed via GA4), increase Meta spend by 20% every 3 days
  • Continue until your blended MER reaches 20% — the "Growth Threshold" where spend is self-funding
  • Formula: MER = Total Revenue ÷ Total Ad Spend. Target: 5–7× during scaling phase
Outcome: Spend scales with mathematical confidence
Critical Watch Point
Do not increase budget by more than 20–25% in a single day. A sudden large budget jump breaks Meta's delivery algorithm and resets the learning phase — costing you 5–7 days of inefficient spend. Slow, disciplined scaling consistently outperforms aggressive budget spikes.
Step 11
The 30-Day Performance Audit
Complexity: Moderate
▼
The Action
On Day 28, compare Week 4 Mobile CVR against the Week 1 baseline. Run a full blended MER audit:
  • If CVR improved (e.g., 1.8% → 2.5%+), increase Meta spend by 15% every 3 days going forward
  • MER safety band: Keep blended MER at 12–15× during scaling. If MER drops below 10×, stop scaling and re-audit mobile checkout
  • Measure replenishment revenue separately to understand true retention economics
Outcome: Data-backed scaling roadmap to ₹20L+
The Bigger Picture
This audit is not the end — it is the beginning of Month 2. If the system has been correctly implemented, you will have: a measured LTV, a proven TOF offer, an automated retention engine, and a protected margin stack. That is the infrastructure for scaling to ₹20L with confidence.
